Meeting Roles


TOASTMASTER
Job: The Toastmaster is the meeting's director and host. A member typically will not be assigned this role until they are thoroughly familiar with the club and its procedures. As Toastmaster, you:
  • Acquire a meeting agenda from your Vice President Education.
  • Work with the General Evaluator to ensure all club participants know their roles and responsibilities.
  • Introduce speakers during the club meeting, including their speech topic, project title, objectives, delivery time, etc. during your introduction.
  • Ensure smooth transitions between speakers during the club meeting.


    • AH COUNTER
    Job: The AH COUNTER will make note of any inappropriate words or sounds or pause fillers that distract the audience¡¯s attention from the speaker¡¯s message.

    Whose speech will be accountable? Anyone who speaks during the meeting is accountable. The AH COUNTER must listen to everyone who speaks during the meeting and record how many ¡°AHs¡± were spoken.



    GRAMMARIAN
    Job: The GRAMMARIAN role is truly an exercise in expanding your listening skills. You have three basic responsibilities:
  • Comment on the correct use of the English language during the course of the meeting and then provide constructive feedback. All speakers are subject to the rules of English so please make sure you listen to everyone that is speaking.
  • Comment on any good usage of the English language.
  • Comment how many times the Word or Phrase of the Day was mentioned during the meeting.

    Whose speech will be accountable? Anyone who speaks during the meeting is accountable.



    • TIMER
    Job:The TIMER is the club member that makes sure that all meeting role takers start and finish their duties within a time limit. Each segment of the meeting is timed and the results given to the club in an oral report.
  • The time limits for each speaker must be known.
  • Raise the GREEN card when the speaker has spoken for the minimum amount of time. Please keep the card raised and visible. This is the minimum amount of time required to qualify for an award.
  • Raise the YELLOW card and lower the GREEN when the speaker has reached the midpoint of the presentation. The speaker should start wrapping up the speech.
  • Raise the RED card and lower the YELLOW card when the speaker has reached the maximum amount of time allowed.
  • Continue to raise the RED card until the speaker finishes. A grace period of 30 seconds is allowed.
  • Record the speaker¡¯s name and time spoken
